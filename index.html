Learn BMAD

12 Modules · Pick Any Order · Hands-On in Claude Code · Bring Your Own Build

Understand the BMAD Method — without drowning in setup.

BMAD (Build More, Architect Dreams) is an AI-driven way to build software that plans first and uses specialist agents instead of one AI winging it in a single chat. This course teaches the why and the workflow — grounded in the one real thing you want to build.

Bring your own build. At setup you describe the one real thing you want to make. Every module applies a BMAD idea to it — and the capstone turns it into a one-page plan you can actually use.

  • Open the folder in Claude Code and say "Let's get started"
  • Tell the instructor what you want to build
  • Pick any module — it's a buffet, not a staircase — and apply it to your build
Get Started

Who This Course Is For

For people learning to build — not senior engineers. If you've felt the chaos of "just ask the AI and hope," this gives you a calm, repeatable scaffolding instead.

  • Explain why BMAD beats one-chat "vibe coding"
  • Name the four phases and what each is for
  • Decide how much process a job actually needs
  • Map your own build onto the BMAD workflow
No coding, agile, or product-management background needed. Truly beginner-first — jargon gets defined the moment it shows up.

What You'll Learn

The Why

The five beliefs behind BMAD, and why winging it in one chat falls apart on real builds.

The Four Phases

Analysis → Planning → Solutioning → Implementation: what each is for and what it produces.

Your AI Crew

Specialist agents (analyst, PM, architect, dev) and Party Mode — a team, not a generalist.

Right-Sizing

Quick-dev vs the full method — and the first-class skill of knowing when NOT to use heavy process.

About This Course

Built for beginners who want a solid, repeatable way to build with AI — instead of throwing a vague request at one chat and hoping.

It's a buffet: 12 short, standalone modules you can take in any order, each grounded in the one real thing you want to build.

Content is distilled faithfully from the official BMAD Method documentation. It's an independent learning aid, not an official BMAD product — when in doubt, it points you to the real docs rather than guessing.
